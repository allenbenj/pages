Lunar Illumination & Nighttime Visibility

Photometric conditions at approximately 70% lunar illumination (Waxing/Waning Gibbous Phase)

Under a lunar illumination level of approximately 70%, the moon provides a significant amount of natural ambient light during nighttime hours. This illumination corresponds to the waxing or waning gibbous phase, when the majority of the moon's surface is reflecting sunlight toward Earth.

In a non-urban outdoor environment such as a residential area, rural setting, or open field, this light level permits clear visual orientation. A person with unimpaired vision can readily discern large physical objects—trees, buildings, parked vehicles, and stationary persons—at distances exceeding 50 feet.

Moonlight at this intensity reduces deep shadow formation and outlines major shapes with a soft silvery contrast. Ground surface variations (grass vs. gravel), roadways, and natural paths remain visually distinguishable without artificial light.

A person could traverse a yard, driveway, street, or open field without a flashlight. Curbs, steps, and basic obstructions would remain visible, and navigation would not require tactile guidance unless a visual impairment existed.

Facial recognition at close range (approximately 10–15 feet) would be possible. Beyond that range, body silhouette, posture, and movement would remain visibly detectable.

While colors appear muted under moonlight, high-contrast objects—such as light-colored clothing against darker surroundings—remain clearly distinguishable.
