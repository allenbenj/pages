"What I fear most is power with impunity. I fear abuse of power, and the power to abuse." — Isabel Allende


The justice system does not collapse in a single moment — it erodes slowly when truth is negotiated, misconduct is tolerated, and those sworn to uphold justice choose silence over accountability.


The prosecutor's duty is not to secure convictions, but to seek justice. The investigator’s role is not to build a case at any cost, but to uncover the truth without distortion or bias. When that duty is betrayed — whether by concealing evidence, suborning perjury, misleading the court, or turning a blind eye to conflicts of interest — it is not merely the defendant’s rights that are imperiled, but the moral foundation of the judicial process itself.


This is not just a procedural challenge. It is a call to reaffirm what is core to all Americans: the belief that truth matters, that process protects, and that power must answer to principle.

Brady Violation
Burying Brady — One Held the Shovel; Another Held Their Tongue

The act of suppression did not occur in isolation; it required both the deliberate concealment of material facts and the conscious decision of another actor to withhold disclosure in clear defiance of § 15A-903 and the constitutional duty to ensure a fair adjudicative process.

This dual misconduct forms a single constitutional injury: the State deprived the defense of information it was unequivocally entitled to, impairing investigation, shaping an inaccurate factual narrative, and corrupting the truth-seeking function of the tribunal. When evidence is buried and silence fortifies the grave, the law demands not only exhumation, but accountability for each hand involved in the burial.

When familial interest infiltrates a law enforcement officer’s investigation, neutrality does not bend—it breaks. What remains is a process emptied of constitutional integrity, its foundation shifted from fact-finding to protection of kin. Such a deviation is not a flaw to be weighed; it is a structural failure that invalidates the enterprise itself. The Constitution does not tolerate justice administered under the shadow of personal allegiance or personal profit.
